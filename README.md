# Housing Discrimination and Algorithms Bias Analysis.

## Overview

This project examines the way past housing discrimination is still affecting current mortgage lending trends. It is basing the analysis on Hennepin County, although with additional information about Ramsey County, Minnesota (2023).

Racial covenants (1910-1955) limited the property ownership of the minorities. Even though the Fair Housing Act of 1968 prohibited these practices, the impact of such actions is still present in the modern housing and lending statistics.

This project is aimed at investigating the presence of inequalities in the past and how these manifest in current statistics and whether contemporary systems replicate bias unintentionally.

---

## Objectives

- Study housing variables including: property value, income, neighborhood composition: data bias.  
- Check the presence of algorithmic bias in the decision on mortgage approvals based on various demographics.  
- Name and Find proxy variables that indirectly bring bias to the decision-making systems.  

---

## Datasets Used

- **Mortgage Covenant Dataset**  
  Mortgage applications, historical covenant and census data as a pre-processed dataset.  

- **HMDA Dataset (2023)**  
  Used when there is long-term analysis that is to be done, especially in the case of Ramsey County.  

---

## Key Analysis Areas

- Housing market distribution of property values based on covenant density.  
- Rates of mortgage approvals based on the race and the percentage of the minority population.  
- Hennepin and Ramsey county comparison.  
- Proxy bias is identified using such variables as income, the location and the value of the property.  

---

## Key Findings

- The rate of mortgage approval approaches the negative with minority population.  
- Historical patterns of covenants affect the value of property and the income levels.  
- There are apparent differences in the approval rates by race.  
- The same trends in the two counties indicate bigger systemic tendencies.  

---

## Ethical Perspective

This project presents fairness, transparency and accountability as key elements of data-driven systems. Although proxy variables do not necessarily apply when dealing with sensitive factors such as race, they may still be biased.

---

## Repository Contents

---

## Conclusion

The discussion reveals that past housing discrimination still has effects to the contemporary lending systems. It stresses on the importance of data analysis and using algorithms cautiously to make fair decisions.
